From initial concepts to detailed CAD models, the design phase of ANZU was driven by a single goal: maximum payload capacity with minimum drag.

The structural design of the quadrotor frame utilizes a **lightweight carbon fiber composite**. This material choice was non-negotiable, offering an incredible strength-to-weight ratio that maximizes flight time and payload capacity.

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

To optimize the shape of the arms and fuselage, we ran extensive **Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations**. These tests helped us iterate on the design to reduce turbulence and energy waste, ensuring efficient cruising speed and battery life.

The final frame design is modular, allowing for quick field repairs and interchangeability of different sensor mounting brackets. This flexibility is what makes ANZU a true multi-mission platform.
